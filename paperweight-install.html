Windows Setup
Windows 10 or 11 · Uses WSL2 under the hood
~15 minutes
📋
Before you start — you'll need:
  • Windows 10 (version 2004 or later) or Windows 11
  • At least 2 GB of free disk space
  • Your media files somewhere on your computer
1
Download the installer
Click the Windows download button at the top of this page. A file called paperweight-v1.0-win.exe will download to your Downloads folder.
ℹ️
Windows may show a "Windows protected your PC" warning when you run the file. This is normal for new software. Click "More info" then "Run anyway".
2
Run the installer
Double-click paperweight-v1.0-win.exe. The installer will set up WSL2 (Windows Subsystem for Linux) automatically if it isn't already installed. This is what lets Paperweight run on Windows — you don't need to know anything about it.
⚠️
If WSL2 needs to be installed, your computer will restart once. Save anything you're working on before clicking through. After the restart, run the installer again to continue.
3
Choose your vault folder
The installer will ask you to select a folder where your media lives — beats, podcasts, videos, whatever you make. This becomes your Paperweight vault. You can change it later from the dashboard.
💡
Not sure which folder to pick? Your Music folder, Downloads, or wherever you keep your project files all work fine. Paperweight reads your files but never moves or deletes them.
4
Let it scan
Paperweight will scan your vault folder and index your files. A progress bar shows how it's going. Depending on how many files you have, this takes between 30 seconds and a few minutes.
5
Your station is live
Once the scan finishes, Paperweight opens your station in your browser automatically at localhost:8080. You'll hear your content already playing. The creator dashboard is at localhost:8080/dashboard.
✅
It worked if you can hear your content playing in the browser and see your files listed in the library. If the browser doesn't open automatically, open Chrome or Firefox and go to localhost:8080.
🎙️
You're on air.
Your station is broadcasting at localhost:8080. To make it accessible to others, you'll need to set up port forwarding on your router — here's a simple guide for that. Or just share the link inside your local network for now.
Common issues
The installer says WSL2 isn't supported on my computer ▼
WSL2 requires Windows 10 version 2004 (May 2020) or later. Go to Settings → System → About and check your Windows version. If it's older, run Windows Update first. If you're on Windows 10 Home, WSL2 is still supported — no upgrade needed.
Browser opens but I hear nothing / see no files ▼
Your vault folder might be empty or contain file types Paperweight doesn't recognize yet. Supported formats are MP3, WAV, FLAC, AAC, MP4, MOV. Try pointing it at a folder you know has MP3s or WAVs and re-run the scan from the dashboard.
Paperweight stopped working after my computer restarted ▼
Paperweight doesn't start automatically on boot by default. Find the Paperweight app in your Start menu and launch it. We'll add auto-start as an option in a future update.
Still stuck ▼
Join the Paperweight Discord and drop your question in #setup-help. Include your Windows version and a screenshot of the error if you have one.

macOS Setup
macOS 12 Monterey or later · Intel & Apple Silicon
~10 minutes
📋
Before you start — you'll need:
  • macOS 12 Monterey or later (Intel or Apple Silicon both work)
  • At least 2 GB of free disk space
  • Your media files somewhere on your Mac
1
Download the DMG
Click the macOS download button at the top of this page. A file called paperweight-v1.0-mac.dmg will download to your Downloads folder.
2
Open and install
Double-click the DMG file. Drag the Paperweight icon into your Applications folder when the window appears.
⚠️
macOS may say "Paperweight can't be opened because it's from an unidentified developer." To fix this: go to System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down, and click "Open Anyway" next to the Paperweight warning.
3
Launch Paperweight
Open Paperweight from your Applications folder. On first launch it will ask you to select your vault folder — point it at wherever your music, podcasts, or videos live.
💡
Paperweight will ask for permission to access your selected folder. Click Allow when macOS prompts you — this is required for it to read your files.
4
Let it scan and go live
Paperweight scans your vault and starts broadcasting automatically in shuffle mode. Your station opens at localhost:8080 in your default browser. The creator dashboard is at localhost:8080/dashboard.
✅
It worked if you can hear your content playing in the browser. The Paperweight icon will appear in your menu bar — click it any time to open the dashboard or stop the station.
🎙️
You're on air.
Your station is live at localhost:8080. Paperweight runs quietly in your menu bar. To share your station with people outside your home network, follow the port forwarding guide.
Common issues
"Paperweight is damaged and can't be opened" ▼
This is a macOS Gatekeeper issue. Open Terminal (search for it with Spotlight) and run: xattr -cr /Applications/Paperweight.app — then try opening it again. We're working on getting the app notarized to remove this step entirely.
No sound / files not showing up ▼
Make sure your vault folder contains supported formats: MP3, WAV, FLAC, AAC, MP4, MOV. Also check that Paperweight has permission to access the folder — go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → Files and Folders.
Still stuck ▼
Join the Paperweight Discord and drop your question in #setup-help with your macOS version and what happened.

Ubuntu / Debian Setup
Ubuntu 20.04, 22.04, 24.04 · Debian 11 / 12
~10 minutes
📋
Before you start — you'll need:
  • Ubuntu 20.04 or later, or Debian 11 or later
  • Access to a terminal window
  • At least 2 GB of free disk space
1
Open a terminal
Press Ctrl + Alt + T to open a terminal window. You'll see a blinking cursor waiting for a command. That's all you need.
ℹ️
To paste commands into the terminal, use Ctrl + Shift + V (not Ctrl+V). Then press Enter to run them.
2
Download and run the installer
Copy and paste this command into your terminal, then press Enter:
Terminal
$ curl -fsSL https://paperweight.fm/install | bash
The installer handles everything — Node.js, FFmpeg, and Paperweight itself. It'll tell you what it's doing as it goes.
3
Choose your vault folder
The installer will ask: "Where are your media files?" Type the path to your folder and press Enter. If you're not sure, your home folder path looks like /home/yourname/Music.
💡
Not sure what your folder path is? Type pwd in the terminal while you're inside the folder to see its exact path.
4
Start Paperweight
Once installation finishes, start your station:
Terminal
$ paperweight start
→ scanning vault...
→ broadcast live at localhost:8080
Open your browser and go to localhost:8080. Your station is live.
✅
It worked if you can hear your content in the browser. Run paperweight stop to turn off the station when you're done.
🎙️
You're on air.
Station live at localhost:8080 · Dashboard at localhost:8080/dashboard. To run Paperweight automatically on startup, run paperweight enable. To share your station outside your home network, follow the port forwarding guide.
Common issues
"curl: command not found" ▼
Run sudo apt install curl first, then try the install command again. Enter your password when asked — the cursor won't move while you type, that's normal.
Permission denied errors during install ▼
Add sudo before the install command: sudo curl -fsSL https://paperweight.fm/install | bash. You'll be prompted for your password.
Still stuck ▼
Join the Paperweight Discord — #setup-help. Copy the error message from your terminal and paste it there.

Raspberry Pi Setup
Pi 4 or Pi 5 · Raspberry Pi OS 64-bit (Bookworm)
~20 minutes
📋
Before you start — you'll need:
  • Raspberry Pi 4 (4GB RAM recommended) or Pi 5
  • Raspberry Pi OS 64-bit installed on an SD card (16GB minimum)
  • A USB drive or external hard drive with your media files
  • Your Pi connected to your home network via ethernet or Wi-Fi
ℹ️
New to Raspberry Pi? If you haven't set up your Pi yet, start here — the official Raspberry Pi Imager makes it straightforward. Come back once you have a working Pi on your network.
1
Open a terminal on your Pi
If you have a monitor connected, press Ctrl + Alt + T. If you're connecting from another computer via SSH, open your terminal app and connect with:
Your computer's terminal
$ ssh pi@raspberrypi.local
💡
If raspberrypi.local doesn't work, find your Pi's IP address from your router's device list and use that instead, e.g. ssh pi@192.168.1.42.
2
Plug in your media drive
Connect your USB drive containing your media files to the Pi. To find where it's mounted, run:
Terminal
$ lsblk
→ look for your drive — usually /media/pi/[drivename]
3
Run the installer
Paste and run this in the terminal:
Terminal
$ curl -fsSL https://paperweight.fm/install | bash
On a Pi 4, this takes about 3–5 minutes. The Pi is slower than a laptop — that's fine, let it finish.
4
Point it at your vault and start
Tell Paperweight where your media is, then start the station:
Terminal
$ paperweight start --vault /media/pi/YOURDRIVE/music
→ replace YOURDRIVE with your drive name
5
Open your station from any device
Your Pi's IP address is your station address. Find it by running hostname -I, then open a browser on any device on your home network and go to: http://[your-pi-ip]:8080
✅
It worked if you can open the station from your phone or laptop browser. To make the station run automatically every time the Pi powers on, run paperweight enable.
🎙️
Your Pi is a broadcast station.
Runs 24/7 on about 5 watts of power. Dashboard at http://[pi-ip]:8080/dashboard. To open your station to the internet (not just your home network), follow the port forwarding guide.
Common issues
Installer is very slow or seems frozen ▼
The Pi 4 is genuinely slower at installing software — give it up to 10 minutes before worrying. If there's been no output for over 10 minutes, press Ctrl+C and try again. Make sure your Pi is connected via ethernet rather than Wi-Fi for a faster install.
Can't find my drive with lsblk ▼
Try unplugging and replugging the drive, then run lsblk again. If it still doesn't show up, try a different USB port. The Pi 4 has both USB 2.0 and USB 3.0 (blue) ports — the blue ones are faster.
Station works on my home network but not from the internet ▼
That's expected. By default Paperweight is only accessible on your local network. To open it to the internet you'll need to set up port forwarding on your router. Full guide here.
Still stuck ▼
Join the Paperweight Discord — #setup-help. Tell us your Pi model, OS version, and paste the error message.
